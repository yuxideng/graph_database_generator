<TD ALIGN="left" VALIGN="top"><B>Entry into a Material Definitive Agreement. </B></TD></TR></TABLE> <P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><I>Underwritten Public Offering of Common Stock </I></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">On March&nbsp;16, 2017, Tesla,&nbsp;Inc. (the &#147;<U>Company</U>&#148;) entered into an underwriting agreement (the &#147;<U>Common Stock Underwriting
Agreement</U>&#148;) with Goldman, Sachs&nbsp;&amp; Co., Deutsche Bank Securities Inc., Citigroup Global Markets Inc. and Morgan Stanley&nbsp;&amp; Co. LLC, as representatives of the underwriters (the &#147;<U>Underwriters</U>&#148;) to issue and
sell 1,335,878 shares of common stock, par value $0.001 per share (the &#147;<U>Common Stock</U>&#148;), of the Company in a public offering pursuant to a Registration Statement on Form <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">S-3</FONT> (File <FONT
STYLE="white-space:nowrap">No.&nbsp;333-211437)</FONT> (the &#147;<U>Registration Statement</U>&#148;) and a related prospectus, including the related prospectus supplement, filed with the Securities and Exchange Commission (the &#147;<U>Common
Stock Offering</U>&#148;). In addition, the Company granted the Underwriters an option to purchase, for a period of 30 calendar days from March&nbsp;16, 2017, up to an additional 200,381 shares of Common Stock. The Company estimates that the net
proceeds from the Common Stock Offering will be approximately $347.4&nbsp;million, or approximately $399.6&nbsp;million if the Underwriters exercise in full their option to purchase the additional shares of Common Stock, after deducting underwriting
discounts and estimated offering expenses. The Common Stock Underwriting Agreement is filed as Exhibit 1.1 to this Current Report on Form <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">8-K</FONT> and is incorporated herein by reference. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><I>Underwritten Public Offering of 2.375% Convertible Senior Notes due March&nbsp;15, 2022 </I></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">On March&nbsp;16, 2017, the Company entered into an underwriting agreement (the &#147;<U>Note Underwriting Agreement</U>&#148;) with the Underwriters to issue
and sell $850.0&nbsp;million aggregate principal amount of 2.375% Convertible Senior Notes due March&nbsp;15, 2022 (the &#147;<U>Notes</U>&#148;) in a public offering pursuant to the Registration Statement and a related prospectus, including the
related prospectus supplement, filed with the Securities and Exchange Commission (the &#147;<U>Note Offering</U>&#148;). In addition, the Company granted the Underwriters an option to purchase, for a period of 30 calendar days from March&nbsp;16,
2017, up to an additional $127.5&nbsp;million aggregate principal amount of Notes solely to cover over-allotments. The Company estimates that the net proceeds from the Note Offering will be approximately $839.8&nbsp;million, or approximately
$965.9&nbsp;million if the Underwriters exercise in full their option to purchase the additional Notes, after deducting underwriting discounts and estimated offering expenses. The Note Underwriting Agreement is filed as Exhibit 1.2 to this Current
Report on Form <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">8-K</FONT> and is incorporated herein by reference. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">The Notes will be issued pursuant to a supplemental
indenture to be entered into supplementing the Indenture, dated May&nbsp;22, 2013, by and between the Company and U.S. Bank, National Association, as trustee. </P> <P STYLE="font-size:18pt;margin-top:0pt;margin-bottom:0pt">&nbsp;</P>
